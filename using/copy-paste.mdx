Fontcy turns any text you type into 18 different Unicode styles and lets you copy any of them to your clipboard with a single click. There's nothing to install — just open the page in your browser, type, and copy.

## Using Fontcy step by step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Fontcy in your browser">
    Navigate to the Fontcy page in any modern browser. No account, sign-up, or installation required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type your text in the input box">
    Click the textarea at the top of the page and start typing. Fontcy renders a live preview of all 18 styles as you type, so you can see every option update in real time. The default text is "The Quick Brown Fox" — replace it with whatever you'd like to style.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the style you want in the grid">
    Scroll through the grid of font boxes below the input. Each box shows your text in a different Unicode style. The grid automatically adjusts to fill the available width.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the box to copy">
    Click any font box to copy its Unicode text to your clipboard instantly. A **COPIED** badge appears at the top of the box for one second to confirm the copy succeeded. The text is now ready to paste.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste anywhere">
    Go to your target platform — a social media bio, a message thread, an email — and paste as you normally would (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V). Because Fontcy copies plain Unicode text, it pastes as text rather than an image or attachment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Where to use your styled text

Fontcy's output is standard Unicode text, so it works anywhere that accepts Unicode input. The following platforms support styled text in most contexts:

- **Twitter/X** — bios, display names, and tweets
- **Instagram** — bios and post captions
- **Discord** — usernames, server names, and messages
- **LinkedIn** — headlines and about sections
- **Facebook** — posts and display names
- **Reddit** — posts and usernames (support varies by style)
- **Email** — subjects and body text
- **SMS and messaging apps** — most modern apps display Unicode characters correctly

<Info>
  Unicode text is plain text — it pastes as text, not an image. The recipient doesn't need any special font installed to see the styled characters.
</Info>

<Warning>
  Some platforms strip or replace unusual Unicode characters. Test your styled text on the target platform before committing to it for a permanent bio.
</Warning>

## Mobile use

Fontcy works in any modern mobile browser — no app download needed. Open the Fontcy page on your phone, type or paste your text into the input box, and tap a font box to copy it. Then switch to your app and paste as usual.
